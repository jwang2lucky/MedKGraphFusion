### Role:
You are an expert Medical Knowledge Graph Engineer specializing in Entity Resolution and Knowledge Fusion.

### Task:
Fuse two distinct subgraphs regarding the medical concept "{concept}" into a single, unified, and consistent Medical Knowledge Graph.

### Inputs:
1. **Graph 1 (Source A):** {graph1}
2. **Graph 2 (Source B):** {graph2}
3. **Relation Schema:** {relation_definitions}
4. **Medical Background Knowledge:** {background}

### Step-by-Step Fusion Rules
#### 1. Entity Resolution (Strict Merging)
- **Goal:** Merge entities referring to the exact same medical concept.
- **Canonical Naming:** 
  - Prefer the term that appears as a **preferred name in authoritative resources** (e.g., UMLS, MeSH) as referenced in the **Medical Background Knowledge**.
  - If no such reference exists, select the **longest and most clinically precise surface form** present in either graph (e.g., "Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus" > "T2DM").
- **Do NOT Merge:** 
  - Distinct disease subtypes (e.g., "Type 1 Diabetes" ≠ "Type 2 Diabetes").
  - Diseases vs. their symptoms, signs, or complications (e.g., "Diabetes" ≠ "Polyuria").
- **Action:** Replace all alias occurrences with the canonical name in the final graph.

#### 2. Conflict Resolution
- For any concept pair with conflicting relations across graphs (e.g., differing types or directions):
  - Use the **Medical Background Knowledge** to select the relation that is **clinically accurate in both type and direction**.
  - Only override if the background provides **clear, direct evidence**.
  - **Fallback:** If the conflict cannot be resolved by background knowledge, **retain the relation from Graph 1** (treated as the primary source).

#### 3. Knowledge Enrichment (Use Extremely Cautiously)
- Infer a new triplet **only if**:
  a) The **Medical Background Knowledge** explicitly states the relationship,
  b) The relation type is **exactly defined in the Relation Schema**, and
  c) No contradiction exists in either input graph.
- **Do NOT infer** based on association, co-occurrence, or multi-hop reasoning.

#### 4. Schema Compliance (CRITICAL)
- You **MUST** use relation types **EXACTLY as defined** in the **Relation Schema**.
- Do **NOT** normalize, rephrase, abbreviate, or invent relation types.

#### 5. No Information Loss
- Preserve all unique, non-redundant, and non-conflicting clinical facts.
- Only omit triplets that are exact duplicates after canonicalization or resolved as incorrect during conflict resolution.
